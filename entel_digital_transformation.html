How can a team of 700 people deliver systems, e-commerce, and new business rules solutions to guarantee the best consumer experience, and still apply User Centered Design in order to define KPIs? That's the scenario at Entel today. They are restructuring all departments to ensure the best business solutions and experiences. We needed to redefine all digital assets, consumer journeys, and the visual design of e-commerce and applications.


All the UX team was comprised of professionals focused in Information Architecture, Visual Design, and Business Analysis. Together, our mission was to deliver the best experience for all Entel’s digital assets, such as the websites and applications.

About

With a great team of UX professionals, we started to think lean. The work has been done by steps. We made workshop sessions with all the project stakeholders, interviewed users, and collected all the back-end data to start the information architecture. In order to deliver consistency between the screens and the flows, we used a modular design with desktop and mobile patterns. The e-commerce solution was aiming to increase sales and improve online conversions, so the UX team worked hard to make a bridge between the business areas with the technology area, as the legacy systems are still in use.


We adopted the following process:

 

- Creation of usage cases based on the client’s expertise;

- Flowcharts and processes diagrams with the support of new and legacy systems team;

- User flows reflecting usage cases;

- Information architecture and mockups of all usage cases;

- UI Design and flow prototyping;

The

Solution

The work routine was composed of many conversations, interviews, and alignment between the stakeholders, product analysts, and the technology and development team. We made lots of navigation flow analyses, prototyping, and remodeling of all Entel’s EB2C and B2B products, making room for a complete experience of online selling.

According to benchmarks and research we conducted, onboarding was a key experience problem. We created several usage cases where the user could have a positive and successful experience regardless of the purchase situation or point of contact with an Entel product. The main cases were: portability and data package purchase.

 

When a user inserts a new Entel Chip, a screen and a browser automatically appears, with options where the user can choose what he wants to do. We created a main page with the main tasks.

Experience

for new customers

One of the biggest changes to improve the user experience was the loyalty program establishment. We created a complete ecosystem based on benefits and integration with e-commerce points. In the dashboard, it is possible to purchase, change, and transfer points, allowing the user to have more options with the available rewards. Importantly, all we did was based on Mobile First.
