Ask for a Towing

Tutorial

The Car Insurance area is the SulAmérica’s area with most operations. We had the challenge of redesigning the whole service experience, both in desktop and mobile, bringing innovation and effectiveness in every steps of the user journey.

 

One of the main challenges was the team integration, because we had to maintain consistency with three companies working together: CI&T, IBM, and MJV. We have used the Scrum methodology to maintain control and TI governance. Moreover, we also had to decrease the number of Call Center calls, tickets, and claims, making tasks simpler to the user so he could solve basic issues in the website or the application.

The

Project

The project was to rethink the user experience in services such as towing, insurance claim, or asking for help in emergency situations. We used Design Thinking and Prototyping to design new features and services for the users.

The solution was found via Design Thinking and User Research. Various research methods were used, such as in-depth interviews, ethnography, and surveys. In order to collect rich information, we created different approaches for each of the following:

 

- Brokers

- Stakeholders

- Users

- Area managers and directors

- Development team

 

After thorough data analysis, we worked with some insights that served as resources to run co-creation workshops.

The

Solution

The most powerful ideas were “The Red Button” and the “99 Towing”. These ideas are basically features that allow the user to request an automobile towing and to file a claim notice through the application. They were key ideas to accomplish one of our main challenges – reducing the number of call center calls and helping the user in emergency situations.

Design Thinking Workshop

Mind map with ideas grouped by context

After the workshop, or main resource was a list of insights and ideas that would better align with the business and an optimal experience. Then, we applied an idea validation tool named Value Matrix. This matrix provides the correlation between the difficulty (which takes into account factors such as the idea development complexity) and the aggregate value of the idea for the business. In order to make this analysis, we had support from the development team and the stakeholders.

Survey results

We tracked the user profiles and developed “personas”, that is, general profiles describing users according to their technology adoption level and passion for automobiles.

Personas

We validated the personas with the results from the user research. We then identified patterns in order to create a different user journey for each of the personas. This journey involves the insurance contract, usage, and other steps related to car insurance.

With this material, we conducted the Workshop for more than 20 executives from the company in their company matrix, located in Rio de Janeiro, RJ, Brazil. This workshop was a huge Design Thinking session comprised of co-creation, idea validation, and brainstorming activities.

User journey with all profiles. In the image above, the claim notice journey is depicted.

Sitemap

On the basis of the study results, we identified that the best way to provide this type of service was through mobile devices, such as smartphones and smartwatches. Thus, we initiated the iPhone and Apple Watch prototype development. With SulAmérica’s brand guidelines in mind, the focus was on providing something clean, simple, and without noises, while also trying to make the user feel as calm as possible during emergency situations.
