The challenge was to transform a legacy system (without a mobile version) in a digital asset in which the investor could execute everything he needs on a daily basis, without having to depend on phone calls to the customer service. A very thorough understanding of the user was essential.

Main

Challenges

The

Project

SulAmérica Seguros is one of the largest insurance companies in Brazil. The Investments and Welfare area was not implementing good UX practices to its clients. User and stakeholder interviews, design thinking workshops, and prototyping helped to create a new product for this area.

The solution was found via Design Thinking. Since the Design Thinking process is usually long, I created a 4-hour workshop with co-creation techniques in order to make the immersion in a timely manner.

 

Beyond that, we identified that a desktop software did not make sense for this user. Hence, the solution was to develop a native application with the Adobe PhoneGap technology in order to provide the same content across different platforms.

The

Solution

The creation of the investments application was made after five usability and concept test trials with two groups of users: users with brand familiarity (SulAmérica users) and users that never had any contact with the brand. I have developed the application interfaces focusing in all insights we received during the workshops and interview sessions. 

The

Investments

Application

Value Matrix

The main functionality required by users was the display of daily profitability charts. We tested different simulations of push notifications and profitability displays, overall account balance, and other features, always focusing on providing interactive charts.

Design Thinking and Co-Creation Workshop

Investment App Screens

Graphic details and account transactions

The ‘Contact Us’ and ‘Sign In’ features were made in such a way that the user would have a stress-free and a fast response. Examples of these features include: (i) biometric authentication, (ii) accessible phone numbers, and (iii) password and account activation inside the application, removing the burden of doing this through a website or an e-mail link.

After the workshop, we had a list with ideas and insights that was in alignment with the business and with an optimal experience. Then, we applied an idea validation tool named Value Matrix. This matrix provides the correlation between the difficulty (which takes into account factors such as the idea development complexity) and the aggregate value of the idea for the business. In order to make this analysis, we had support from the development team and the stakeholders.

Ideas and Insights list

Profitability index push notification

Having access to the profitability history and account transactions was well requested in the workshops. Since the investors are always in different places, sometimes they are unable to use laptops or computers. Thus, the best way to fulfill this need was providing financial information for each transaction.
